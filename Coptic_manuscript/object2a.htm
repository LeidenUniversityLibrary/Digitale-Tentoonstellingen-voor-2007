Vervolg


Or. 12.330
Coptic, paper (damaged), 7 ff. (in disorder), 4 larger illuminations, also smaller ornaments, possibly of the 17th century. Disparate leaves from what seems to be a Coptic Synaxarium, or a Coptic Lectionary. Lessons from the Gospel of St. Mark, and possibly from other Gospels. Provenance: Purchased by auction from J.L. Beijers, auctioneers in Utrecht, 1970 (catalogue No. 1176).

Or. 14.331
Coptic, parchment, 1 f., a damaged fragment, with largest measurements 15 x 18.5 cm. In uncial writing. The original MS had a lay-out of two columns, and remains of the second column can be seen at the right. Fragment of the (apocryph) Acta Jacobi Zebedaei, the Acts of the Apostle James, in the Sahidic dialect. Added are notes by Erik von Scherling of Oegstgeest (d. 1956), the former owner, the noted Swedish collector and dealer of early texts from this area. Von Scherling’s notes include a full transcription of the Coptic text and a comparative survey of other texts. Estimate confirmed by Dr. A. Klasens, then (1975) director of the National Museum of Antiquities in Leiden, to date from c. 500 AD (personal information). Provenance: Purchased in November 1975 from Mr. Laurence Witten, Monroe (USA).

Or. 14.404
Collective volume with texts in (Bohairic) Coptic and Arabic, paper, 81 ff., illuminations, dated 29 Amshir 1360 (Era of the Martyrs / 1640 AD), copied in Jerusalem by the archdeacon Salih b. Girgis al-Diwan, Oriental binding with flap, boards pasted with silk. Colophon with waqf-note (donated to the Church of the Holy Sepulchre in Jerusaqlem) on f. 68a. All texts have a connection with St. John the Baptist. (1) ff. 1a-17a. Arabic and Coptic. Turuhat. (2) ff. 18a-27a. Arabic and Coptic. Fusul Mukhtassa bi-`Id Mari Yuhanna al-Ma`madani. (3) ff. 27b-35a. Arabic and Coptic. Collection of readings for the feast of St. John the Baptist. (4) ff. 35b-44a. Arabic and Coptic. Fusul Mukhtassa bi-`Id Mari Yuhanna al-Ma`madani. (5) ff. 44b-55b. Arabic and Coptic. Fusul Mukhtassa bi-`Id Mari Yuhanna al-Ma`madani. Readings for the 2nd day of the month Tut. (6) ff. 67b-56a. Arabic translation of the Homily (Mimar) on the martyrdom of St. John the Baptist, for the 2nd day of the month Tut, by Johannes Chrysostomus (354-407), see Graf, GCAL I, 337. (7) ff. 80b-68b. Arabic version of a homily (Mimar) on the birth of St. John the Baptist, for the 30th day of the month Ba’una, by Ya`qub al-Sarugi (d. 521 AD), see Graf, GCAL I, 444. See J.J. Witkam, Catalogue of Arabic Manuscripts [...], pp. 484-494 (with help of Dr. J. van der Vliet). Earlier provenances: Notes in Spanish point to a late 18th-century Spanish owner. Then Thomas (or Arnold?) Rodd, from whom it was purchased by Sir Thomas Phillips (1792-1872). Then MS 4227 in the Phillips collection, then the Robinson Trust, London. Then sold by auction by Sotheby’s, London, on November 27, 1974 (catalogue No. 638). Purchased by the Library in January 1977 from Messrs. E.J. Brill, Leiden.

Or. 14.544
Collective volume with texts in Coptic and Arabic, paper, 248 ff. (f. 93a mistakenly blank), dated Saturday 22 Barmuda 1508 (Era of the Martyrs = 1792 AD, colophons on ff. 174b, 245b). Commissioned by al-Mu`allim Girgis Yusuf Miftah (name written on erasure) and copied by Ibrahim Abu Atbal b. Sham`an al-Khwaniki (colophon on f. 245b). Illuminations (ff. 4a, 175a, strapwork and capitals) and numerous illuminated capitals and smaller ornaments. Full leather (Islamic?) binding with flap, blind tooled ornaments (borders and medallion), possibly not originally belonging to the volume. Contents: (1) ff. 4a-174b. Old Testament. The book Iob. Sifr Ayyub al-Barr. Coptic and Arabic. (2) ff. 175a-245b. Old Testament. Proverbs. Amthal Sulayman, or Sifr Sulayman b. al-Malik Dawud. Coptic and Arabic. Provenance: Purchased in March 1979 from Ms. Sonia Ewart, antiquarian bookseller in London.
Or. 17.084
Coptic, Arabic, paper (loose, damaged, torn), 162 ff., full-leather binding with blind tooled ornaments (borders, medallion), illuminated pages (ff. 2a, 36a, 72b, 127a), numerous illuminated capitals and smaller ornaments, dated Wednesday 28 Misra 1545 (Era of the Martyrs = 1829 AD, colophon on f. 159b). Coptic-Arabic prayerbook, with some lessons from the Scriptures. Coptic and Arabic texts written in two columns, side by side on the page. Also numerous ritual instructions, in Arabic. Provenance: Purchased on October 13, 1980, by auction from Sotheby’s, London (Sotheby’s catalogue No. 90).

Or. 18.139
Coptic, with some Arabic, paper, 216 ff., full leather binding, with blind tooled ornamentation (borders, diagonals), numerous illuminated pages (strapwork, capital, introducing new texts). Coptic Prayer Book, with short Arabic notes and headings. A few texts are bilingual (beginning on ff. 201b, 203a). Abrupt end. Provenance: Purchased by auction on June 20, 1983, from Sotheby’s, London (Sotheby’s catalogue No. 168).

Or. 25.478 - Or. 25.479
Coptic, paper, 2 ff. (11.5 x 8 cm; 29.5 x 21 cm) in passe-partout, 9 lines, 20 lines. Two leaves from unidentified Coptic manuscripts. Text on both sides.
Provenance: Acquired by Robert C. Hekker (1917-1990) in Cairo, in 1973, ‘from an antiquarian bookseller in Qasr al-Nil Street’. Hekker Legacy (1994).
