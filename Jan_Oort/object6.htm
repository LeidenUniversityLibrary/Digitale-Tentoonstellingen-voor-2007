6. Comets

In 1950 Oort published an article in which it was shown that the distribution of comet orbits, or, to be more exact, the distribution of the inverse of the major axes of their orbits, can be explained by assuming a reservoir of comets on the boundaries of the solar system. Disturbances by passing stars will perturb comets out of their roughly circular orbits within the reservoir, and some of them will be led into orbits that take them close to the sun. Once they move through the inner part of the Solar system, they may be trapped into thighter orbits by Jupiter, and become periodic comets, like Halley’s comet.
